Our Community

Built by Hand, Bound by Heart

In the early 1950s, the families of North Harbour came together with a shared vision: to build a place of worship and community in the heart of St. Mary’s Bay. They pooled their resources, purchased the land, and raised Sacred Heart Church with their own hands.

That same spirit lives on today. Our parish isn’t defined by a building — it’s defined by the people who fill it. Neighbours helping neighbours, volunteers giving their time, and a community that has never forgotten the value of kindness, welcoming, and care.

Whether you’re a lifelong parishioner or visiting for the first time, you’re part of this story. North Harbour has always had room for one more.

Heritage & Remembrance

Cemetery Restoration

The Sacred Heart Cemetery is more than a resting place — it’s a record of every family that helped shape North Harbour. Restoring and maintaining these grounds is an act of respect for the generations who came before us.

Much of this restoration work has been carried out by hand, with Terry Power dedicating countless hours of personal labour to clearing, repairing, and honouring these sacred grounds. It’s the kind of quiet, steady commitment that defines this community.

Gravesites Database

Shawn Power has compiled a searchable database of gravesites at Sacred Heart Cemetery — an invaluable resource for families researching their North Harbour roots and heritage.
