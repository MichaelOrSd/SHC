Our Story

"A community does not build a church — a church builds a community."

The story of Sacred Heart Church is, at its heart, the story of the people of North Harbour. In the early 1950s, families along the shores of St. Mary's Bay came together with a singular purpose: to establish a permanent place of worship — a sacred gathering point for generations to come.

Through remarkable collective effort, the community raised the funds necessary to purchase the property and construct the church. There were no large benefactors, no outside institutions to carry the weight. It was neighbour helping neighbour, families pooling modest means, and a shared conviction that this place deserved a church worthy of the faith its people held.

The early years were marked by the same spirit of generous, practical care that North Harbour has always been known for. Work bees, community suppers, and quiet acts of donation built this parish from the ground up. Sacred Heart Church became more than a building — it became the spiritual and social heart of the community.

The Community of North Harbour

North Harbour has long been known for something that cannot easily be put into words but is immediately felt upon arrival: a quality of welcome. Locals call it the "North Harbour gene" — a deep, inherited instinct for kindness, for opening the door, for making a stranger feel at home.

This quality found its expression in how the community cared for its church, its elders, its families, and in recent years, how it has turned its attention to preserving the history of those who came before.

Carried Forward

Today, Sacred Heart Church is governed by a dedicated board of community members committed to preserving its legacy. From the restoration of the cemetery to the stewardship of parish records and documents, the same founding spirit — care, community, continuity — guides every effort.

The church stands as testament to what people can accomplish when they gather not around self-interest, but around something greater than themselves.

Heritage Preservation

Cemetery Restoration

The cemetery at Sacred Heart Church holds the remains of the founding families of North Harbour — the men and women whose labour, faith, and love shaped this community across generations. Caring for this ground is among the most meaningful work the parish undertakes.

Our restoration efforts include the cleaning and preservation of headstones, landscape maintenance, and the ongoing documentation of gravesites. Through careful community effort, we are working to ensure that every marker is legible, every name remembered, and every plot treated with the dignity it deserves.

As part of this work, we have established an online gravesites database — a searchable record of those buried in our cemetery, available to researchers, families, and descendants near and far.

Search the North Harbour Cemetery Gravesites Database — a growing record of our parish cemetery, open to all who seek their roots.
